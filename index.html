Interesting facts about Camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Interesting facts about Chameleon

  • Chameleons live in warm varied habitats from rainforests through to deserts.
  • The chameleons' eyes can rotate and focus separately on 180-degree arcs.
  • Species of chameleon can be as small as 15 mm (0.59 in) or as large as 69 cm (27 in).

Interesting facts about Cicada

  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.
  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.

Interesting facts about Panda

  • The giant panda's scientific name is Ailuropoda melanoleuca, which means "black and white cat-foot".
  • Scientists aren't sure how long pandas live in the wild, but in captivity they live to be around 30 years old.
  • Sadly, these beautiful bears are endangered, and it's estimated that only around 1,000 remain in the wild.

Interesting facts about Butterflies

  • Butterflies have taste receptors on their feet to help them find their host plants and locate food.
  • Butterfly's wings are covered by thousands of tiny transparent scales which reflect light in different colors.
  • Butterflies need an ideal body temperature of about 85 degrees Fahrenheit to fly.

Interesting facts about Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.

Interesting facts about Bears

  • Brown bears are generally loners who will only seek a mate for short periods.
  • Their sense of smell enables them to sniff food at a distance of several kilometres.
  • The brown bear is the largest predator still living on the continent of Europe.
